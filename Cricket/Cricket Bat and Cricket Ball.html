Cricket Bat

A Cricket Bat is a specialised piece of equipment used by batters in the sports of cricket to hit Ball, typically consisting of a cane handle attached to a flat-fronted willow-wood blade.It may also be used by a batter's ground to avoid a run put, by holding the bat and touching to avoid a run out,by holding the bat and touching the ground with it.The length of bat may be no more than 38 inches(96.5cm) and the width no more than 4.25 inches(10.8cm). Its use is first mantoined in 1642.Since 1979, a law change has dicated the cricket bats can only be made from wood.

Cricket Ball

The trajectory of a cricket ball when bowled, through movement in the sir, and off ground, is influenced by the action of bowler and the condition of ball and pitch,while working on the cricket ball to obtain optimal condtion is a key role of fielding side.The principal method through which the batter scores runs is by hitting the ball, with the bat, a run,or by directing the ball through or over the boundry.Cricket ball are harder and heavier then baseballs.Different types of balls (red,pink,white) is used during the matches depending upon the game that you are playing.Injuries and fatalies have been caused by cricket balls during matches.
