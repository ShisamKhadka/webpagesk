Introduction of Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field, at the center of which is a 22-yard(20-meter;66-foot) pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails(small sticks)balanced on three stumps.Two players from the batting team,the striker and non-stricker,stand in front of either wicket holding bats,while one player from the fielding team,the bolwer,bowls the ball toward the stricker's wicket from the opposite end of the pitch. The striker's goal is to hit the bowled ball with the bat and then batting team scoring one run for each of exchanges.Runs are also scored when the abll reaches the boundary of field or when the ball is bowled illegally.

Highest governing Body: International Cricket Council
Nickname: The Gentlemen's Game
First played: 16th century,South East England
